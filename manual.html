Getting started — install on your device

1

Open the link in your browser

Use Safari on iPhone/iPad, or Chrome on Android. Paste the app URL into your browser's address bar.

2

Add to your Home Screen

iPhone / iPad: tap the Share icon (box with arrow) at the bottom of Safari → scroll down and tap Add to Home Screen → tap Add.

Android: tap the three-dot menu in Chrome → tap Add to Home Screen or Install app.

3

Open from your Home Screen

The app launches fullscreen, just like a native app. Your data is stored on your device — private to you.

4

Enable notifications (optional but recommended)

Go to Settings tab → tap Enable reminders. Allow notifications when prompted. You'll get a nudge at 12:00 PM and 4:45 PM daily.

Desktop: Chrome and Edge on Mac/Windows also support Add to Home Screen / Install app from the address bar.

How to log your work

The Log tab is where you spend most of your time. Here's the flow:

1

Select the day

Tap Mon, Tue, Wed, Thu, or Fri. It defaults to today automatically.

2

Pick a category

Choose what kind of work it is. This helps the AI organise your report properly.

3

Log the entry — by voice or typing

Voice: tap the 🎙️ mic button, speak naturally, and the entry saves automatically when you stop.
Typing: type in the text field and tap Add (or press Enter).


The six categories

Design Collab Review Research Blocked Admin

Design

Wireframes, flows, prototypes, Figma work, visual design decisions

Collab

Stakeholder meetings, PM syncs, handoffs to engineering, team discussions

Review

Reviewing other work, getting feedback, critique sessions, design QA

Research

User research, competitor analysis, discovery work, reading docs

Blocked

Anything you're stuck on, waiting for, or that needs escalation

Admin

Meetings, planning, documentation, anything operational

Pro tip: One or two entries per day is enough. You don't need to log everything — just the things you'd want to mention in your weekly update. The AI fills in the structure.

Generating your weekly report

1

Tap "Generate weekly report"

This button appears in the Log tab once you have entries. Usually done on Friday afternoon.

2

Wait ~5 seconds

The AI reads all your entries, groups them by theme, and writes a structured update in your chosen tone.

3

Review and edit

The report appears in the Report tab as an editable text area. Read it, adjust any wording, and it's ready.

4

Send or copy

Enter your Slack channel name (e.g. #design-updates) and tap Send to Slack, or tap Copy to Clipboard and paste it yourself.

Not happy with it? Tap Regenerate for a fresh version. You can also edit the report directly in the text area before sending.

Customising your report format

Go to the Settings tab to control exactly how your reports look.

Report sections

Toggle each section on or off. Only the sections you enable will appear in the report. Default is all five on.

Tone

Formal — structured, appropriate for leadership audiences.
Balanced — confident and clear, the recommended default.
Casual — friendly and conversational, good for close-knit teams.

Custom intro

Add an optional opening line that always appears after the header, e.g. "Solid week across design and collab." Leave blank for no intro.

Settings apply to the next report you generate. If you've already generated one, tap Regenerate to apply the new format.

Notifications

Enable daily reminders in Settings to stay consistent without relying on memory.

Notifications fire at your device's local timezone — no setup needed. If you're travelling, they automatically adjust.

iOS note: Notifications only work when the app is installed to your Home Screen via Safari. They won't work in a regular browser tab on iPhone.

Getting updates

The app updates automatically. When a new version is published, just close and reopen the app (or pull to refresh) and you'll have the latest version. Your data is never affected by updates — it stays on your device.

If something looks broken after an update, try: Settings → Safari/Chrome → Clear Cache. Your journal entries are stored separately and won't be deleted.

Frequently asked questions

Is my data shared with anyone?
No. Your journal entries are stored only on your device in your browser's local storage. Nothing is sent to a server except when you generate a report (your entries are sent to the AI to write the summary) or when you send to Slack.
What happens if I clear my browser data?
Your entries would be deleted since they're stored locally. This is why we recommend installing to your Home Screen and not clearing app data. A future version may add cloud backup.
Can I use this on a laptop too?
Yes. Open the URL in any modern browser. Chrome and Edge on Mac/Windows let you install it as a desktop app too (look for the install icon in the address bar).
How do I start a new week?
Tap "Clear week" in the top right of the Log tab. Tap again to confirm. This clears all entries and the saved report so you can start fresh. Do this every Monday.
Can multiple people use this?
Yes — each person uses their own copy on their own device. Everyone's data is separate and private to their device. You all share the same app URL but not each other's data.
The voice feature isn't working — what do I do?
On iPhone, make sure you're using Safari (not Chrome). On Android, use Chrome. Check that microphone permission is allowed for the site in your browser settings.
